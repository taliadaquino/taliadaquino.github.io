- TALIA D'AQUINO -
WHERE IS HERE?
 
SAY MORE - WRITE LESS
 





























Typography is a curious subject matter to discuss.


There are either those who know, and those who don't. Some graphic designers have a strong, passionate approach; others don’t. There are a few caught in the middle,admiring but not producing. But to what extent can the typographic craft be relied on? Do designers ignore the immerse journey craft-makers take in the with their typographic process? Are designers putting themselves ahead of the message and using typography to overpower the content?

Contemporary designers seek a need to over decorate their typographic work or even over decorate their already well-produced work with typography, resulting in
a cluttered mess.     




This is not good design. An over reliance on typography untimely leads to a misuse, lack of though or unnecessary amount of content over the context. What we need to learn through our practice is faith in the craft of typography. Remember the message of the original design and refine the type to suit a purpose. A lack of understanding and appreciation is resulting in a generation of designers adding unnecessary frills to letterforms.

When we keep creating we also eliminate. To what extent can we keep changing typefaces until there are too many options, so many opportunities they become indistinct to each other. The reliance we have of typefaces and the pressure we put on them to say so much is penetrating. Settling down on the dramatic meanings and definitions behind typefaces may allow the craft to extend more freely. If a picture writes a thousand words; typography is twenty-six pictures to create with. Choosing the right typeface can say all that is necessary.

Realistically we need to remember, why is a new typeface important? Is it different from before? Is it being designed just for the sake of being different? In the urgency to design and explore typography in the contemporary landscape, designers have forgotten the key necessities for good type design.

Eliminate this problem all together and retract the dependence on typography to fix the poor design. Remember the purpose it was crafted for and the benefits when the designer uses it in that form.

Take it easy, take a step back, and take a moment to begin the process again. Through the years of trial and error ever beginning with Guttenberg, we can afford to take a breath. Start from the basics again and remember the use of your design skills before the flood of typography sets into overdrive and becomes the new norm. The craft of typography isn’t created to fix the dull design or even as an afterthought. A rule to follow, remember to consider the message more then the text and body of design. The context is enough before the rigorous dress-up of typography. A statute to follow, focus less on overshadowing the message.

Typography isn’t the core, and it is most definitely not a crutch. Focus on content, then the work of design. Don’t attempt to outdo what has been done before, resulting in an over done practice we are still yet to perfect. With typography being so inquisitive to designers, we need to remember how to place the content carefully.

Remembering the message itself is the key and not to over power this with a reliance on typography.
