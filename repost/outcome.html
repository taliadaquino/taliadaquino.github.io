ARCHIVE OF AN EMPTY BOX































Using the outcome from 'idea three' I explored a Google drive journey. Steaming through ideas such as having the documents spread out through sections, which lacked an order as Google Drive only organised through alphabetizing. I kept in my medium of only using Google as a platform, and ultimately ended in a single page document with my text reposted. Yet in white ink which created a lack in the ability to understand the message unless you're the author, much like Beaulieu only understood why the contents of his box where of great importance. Using Google Docs as my 'sheet of paper' to categorise as a 'document' and Google Drive as my 'box' I had one task left to complete: to send the box. Thinking through what platforms this could be shared on, I wanted to keep the social platform anonymous. Then was created: 'archiveofanemptybox@gmail.com: Derek Beaulieu's new fake email'. Using this I published a link to the Google Drive account through Google Plus and then waited. Receiving only one view and one comment, the experiment wasn't a social phenomenon. Although I did receive my tracking number/ my html link. Resulting in the same outcome that Beaulieu set out to achieve yet on a new medium:

The computer, Google and Marshall McLuhan's insight.
